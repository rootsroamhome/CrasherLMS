Science 7.ESS2.1 Unit 1 — Earth Systems
Earth's Rock Cycle — Building a Model
🎬 Hook

The rock under your feet right now used to be lava. And in a few million years, it might be lava again. The whole Earth is constantly recycling itself — slowly, violently, and in ways that would seem completely impossible if you didn't know the science. Watch this eruption clip and try to imagine what happens to that lava after it cools.

✅ I Can…

I can build a model showing how Earth's materials cycle through processes like melting, weathering, and sedimentation.

🎯 I'll Know I've Got It When…

I can look at a rock and make a reasonable guess about how it formed — and explain what might happen to it next in the cycle.

📚 Choose How to Learn It — pick at least one
  • Watch Crash Course Geography — The Rock Cycle ~12 minutes — covers all three rock types and how they transform Watch on YouTube ↗
  • Read CK-12 — The Rock Cycle (Earth Science, 7th Grade) ~10 min read with diagrams and built-in questions Open on CK-12 ↗
  • Explore USGS Interactive — How Rocks Form and Transform ~10 min — click through each rock type and transformation Explore on USGS ↗
📝 Choose How to Show What You Learned — pick one
  • Draw Draw and label the rock cycle from memory. Include: igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks, plus the processes that connect them (melting, cooling, weathering, pressure, etc.).
  • Write Write a paragraph telling the story of a single piece of granite. What happens to it over millions of years? Take it all the way through at least two full transformations.
  • Teach It Back Explain the rock cycle out loud to a parent, using your drawing as a guide. They should be able to ask "okay so what happens if it gets buried deep underground?" and you can answer without looking anything up.
