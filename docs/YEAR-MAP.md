# HomeSkewl — Year-Long Unit Map (2026–2027, 7th grade)

Six interdisciplinary, self-paced units. Each braids science + world history + ELA around one
essential question, using the Unit 1 template: launch (hook + video + vocab flashcards + KWL) →
paired A/B lessons → hands-on builds → a medium creative project → an ELA story/writing arc →
a local Rogue Valley connection → reflection (KWL close + vocab self-sort + written synthesis +
self-rubric). ~16 cards ≈ 16 sessions ≈ 6 weeks per unit at 2–3 unit sessions/week, alongside
daily math + independent reading. Six units × ~6 weeks fits the 37-week year with review/flex room.

Vertical through-line for the year: **systems and power** — each unit re-asks "what does controlling
this (water, materials, land, trade, risk, energy) let people do?", and each unit deliberately
reuses the surplus→specialization dominoes from Unit 1 so the model compounds.

| # | Unit | Essential question | Science strand | History/SS strand | ELA arc | Local hook |
|---|------|--------------------|----------------|-------------------|---------|------------|
| 1 | **Rivers & the Rise of Civilization** ✅ built | How do rivers shape the way people live — and how do people shape rivers back? | Water cycle, erosion/deposition (MS-ESS2-4, 2-2, part 2-1) | First river civilizations, Mesopotamia | Flood myths (Gilgamesh, Yu), myth writing | The Rogue: Takelma, dams, salmon |
| 2 | **Metals & the Rise of Empires** ✅ built | How does changing what things are made of change who has power? | Atoms, matter, chemical reactions (MS-PS1-1…6) | Bronze/Iron Age trade, monopoly, tribute, Assyria | Forge myths (Hephaestus, Ogun), argument seed | Lithium & the ebike; McDermitt Caldera |
| 3 | **Deep Time & the Restless Earth** | The ground remembers everything — who can read what it wrote? | Rock cycle, plate tectonics, geologic time (MS-ESS2-1, 2-2, 2-3) | How geology set the stage for history (Pompeii/Thera; oral history as evidence — Mt. Mazama in Klamath tradition) | Nonfiction reading: evidence & claims (RI.7.1/7.8); explanatory writing | Table Rocks, Crater Lake, Cascadia |
| 4 | **A Connected World** | What really travels when strangers trade? | Uneven distribution of natural resources (MS-ESS3-1); germs & ecosystems revisit (MS-LS2-1) | Silk Road & Indian Ocean trade, spread of ideas/religions, the Black Death | Research writing (W.7.7): follow one thing that traveled the world | What the Rogue Valley imports/exports; pears, timber, chips |
| 5 | **When the Ground Shifts** | We can't stop disasters — so what CAN we do? | Forecasting natural hazards, designing for them (MS-ESS3-2, MS-ETS1-1) | Turning points: disasters that redirected history; Cascadia 1700 quake (tribal oral records + Japanese tsunami logs) | **Choice novel** (survival/turning-point theme), narrative + theme (RL.7.2) | Cascadia prep, wildfire smoke season, family quake kit |
| 6 | **Power** (capstone) | Where does power actually come from? | Energy transformation, kinetic/potential, circuits (MS-PS3-1, 3-2, 3-5, MS-ETS1-1) | Who controls energy controls the age: muscle → water → coal → oil → batteries (empires echo) | "Your Voice": persuasive capstone (W.7.1) — argue for an energy future, deliver it aloud (SL.7.4) | Ebike teardown/design challenge; Rogue hydropower revisit — full-circle to Unit 1 |

Unit 5's science is intentionally light (one standard) to make room for the choice novel;
Unit 6 is the hands-on capstone and deliberately closes the loop back to rivers (hydropower)
and metals (batteries), so the year ends as one connected argument.

**Coverage check:** every science standard is owned by exactly one unit (with planned revisits),
no standard is orphaned, and the three big ELA writing modes (inform, narrate, argue) each get
seeded early and owned late. Tribal history appears in three units as content, not as a sidebar.
