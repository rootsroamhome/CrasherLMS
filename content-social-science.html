Social Science 6/7.H.CE.1 Unit 1 — Human Origins
Early Humans — Where Did We Come From?
🎬 Hook

About 12,000 years ago, humans figured out something that changed absolutely everything — farming. Before that, every single person on Earth was a hunter or gatherer. No cities. No countries. No schools. This episode of Crash Course traces the biggest turning points in human history from the very beginning. It's a good one.

✅ I Can…

I can identify key turning points in early human history and explain how they transformed the way people lived.

🎯 I'll Know I've Got It When…

I can explain three things early humans figured out that changed everything — without looking at notes. Not just what happened, but why it mattered.

📚 Choose How to Learn It — pick at least one
  • Watch Crash Course Big History — The Origin of Humans ~12 minutes — covers the whole sweep from bipedalism to fire to language Watch on YouTube ↗
  • Read OER Project — Big History Unit 6: Homo sapiens ~15 min read — free, well-written, designed for exactly this age Go to OER Project ↗
  • Read a Book Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind — Yuval Noah Harari. Start with Chapter 1. Your library almost certainly has this, and it reads like a story, not a textbook. Chapter 1 is ~20 pages
📝 Choose How to Show What You Learned — pick one
  • Write Write a short paragraph about which early human turning point you think was most important and why. Make a case — don't just describe it, defend your choice.
  • Draw Draw a timeline of the three biggest turning points in early human history. For each one, add a sentence: what changed, and what would life have looked like before it.
  • Teach It Back Tell a parent: if you could go back in time and watch one moment in early human history, what would it be and why? Give enough detail that they could picture the scene.
